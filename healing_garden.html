Healing Garden Case Study

Made In Figma

The Brief

Healing Garden is a native mobile app for iOS & Android. This application is made for people who want to balance their bodily systems in a natural way and be more connected with our home planet.

Purpose

Due to long lists of side effects, and not knowing what we are actually putting into our bodies, a lot of people now are starting to use herbal remedies to heal themselves. Gaining knowledge about a natural way to manage and improve health also leads to learning about mindfulness. I wanted to create a place where people can find and learn how to be at peace while healing themselves internally and externally with Earth's gifts.

Objective

Users should be able to search for and save plants that fit their needs. I want this app to be a place they can come to, to feel relaxed and calm while gaining knowledge as well.

4 User Testing

Had participants test either prototype-depending on which platform they are most familiar to. They provided exceptional feedback.

Positive
  • Testers enjoyed the popup illustration & encouraged for there to be more throughout the app.
  • Commented that the visuals are all clear and calming.
  • Navigations are easy to understand and are familiar.
Negative
  • Testers shared that the 'explore/mindfulness/ section was overall confusing.
  • Prototype of herbs on home screen faded in and out too fast.
  • Category buttons are too big and confusing.

5 Final Designs

From the feedback, I produced an iteration and made tweaks and changes to my designs.
